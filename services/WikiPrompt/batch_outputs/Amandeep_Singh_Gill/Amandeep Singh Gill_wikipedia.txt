Education and career
[
edit
]
Gill holds a bachelor's degree in Electronics and Electrical Communications from
Panjab University
,
Chandigarh
,
India
.
[
4
]
[
13
]
He was a visiting fellow at
Stanford University
's
Center for International Security and Cooperation
(CISAC) during 2008–2009.
[
13
]
[
14
]
He also holds a PhD from
King's College
,
London
.
[
6
]
[
15
]
His PhD thesis is on Nuclear Learning in Multilateral Forums.
[
16
]
[
13
]
[
17
]
Gill began his diplomatic career in 1992 and served in various capacities in the Indian Embassy in
Tehran
,
Iran
, and the High Commission of India in
Colombo
,
Sri Lanka
. He also served at the Indian Mission to the
Conference on Disarmament
in
Geneva
,
Switzerland
[
5
]
[
8
]
and the Indian Mission to the UN in Geneva. His roles in
New Delhi
,
India
included positions in the Disarmament and International Security Affairs

(DISA) Division and the UN Division. He also headed the DISA Division from 2013 to 2016.
[
13
]
Work
[
edit
]
Gill has written about the impact of
artificial intelligence
(AI) on modern life and the necessity for establishing appropriate regulatory frameworks to ensure AI plays a positive role in the future.
[
18
]
He has talked about the potential impact of AI on the Global South and about India's role in "how AI could play out in the Global South."
[
8
]
[
19
]
Gill believes AI can help accelerate the process of achieving the UN's
Sustainable Development Goals
.
[
20
]

[

Gill is married to Ashma Singh and they have one daughter and one son.
