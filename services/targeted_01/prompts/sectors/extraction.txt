ROLE
You are a precision researcher extracting structured thematic expertise data from biographical source text about international elites.

TASK
Identify every substantive sector or policy area in which {{PERSON_NAME}} has worked, and then determine which sector is most dominant across their career.

PERSON
{{PERSON_NAME}}

SOURCE TEXT
{{CHUNK_TEXT}}

REASONING STEPS
Work through each step before producing your JSON output.

Step 1 — Issue-area identification
  Scan the text for every thematic field, policy area, or substantive sector that
  {{PERSON_NAME}} has worked in or is described as an expert in.
  Evidence of sector engagement includes:
    - Job titles or organizational mandates (e.g., "Director of the Environment Programme")
    - Described research focus or publications (e.g., "published work on poverty alleviation")
    - Roles in sector-specific bodies (e.g., "chaired the health committee")
    - Awards or recognition tied to a sector (e.g., "Nobel Prize in Economics")
  Do NOT include sectors based solely on the sector of a conferring body — the person must
  have substantively worked in that area.
  Only include sectors for {{PERSON_NAME}}.

Step 2 — Evidence linking
  For each sector identified, record the specific evidence from the text: what role, title,
  organization, or described activity connects {{PERSON_NAME}} to that sector?

Step 3 — Prominence assessment
  Based on the breadth and depth of evidence, assess:
    "primary"   — a sector with multiple roles or long-term career presence
    "secondary" — a sector with only one role or brief/peripheral engagement

Step 4 — Taxonomy mapping
  Map each sector to the closest standard label from this list:
    development          — poverty reduction, international development, SDGs, aid policy
    human_rights         — civil liberties, humanitarian law, human rights advocacy
    peace_mediation      — conflict resolution, peacekeeping, mediation, security
    finance              — macroeconomics, banking, monetary policy, fiscal policy
    trade                — international trade, WTO, trade negotiations, customs
    technology           — ICT, digital economy, innovation policy, cybersecurity
    science              — natural sciences, research policy, scientific institutions
    government           — domestic public administration, governance reform, public sector
    diplomacy            — bilateral/multilateral diplomacy, foreign policy, international relations
    international_law    — international legal frameworks, treaty negotiation, arbitration
    environment          — climate, conservation, natural resources, sustainability
    global_health        — public health, disease prevention, health systems, WHO-related
    migration            — migration policy, refugee affairs, diaspora
    education            — education policy, higher education, literacy, pedagogy
    other                — substantive area not captured above
  If a sector spans two categories, choose the most specific match.

GUIDELINES
- Extract sectors for {{PERSON_NAME}} only.
- Do not include sectors merely because an organization {{PERSON_NAME}} worked for operates
  in that area — only include if the person's own role was substantively in that sector.
- supporting_quote must be a verbatim extract of 5–30 words from the SOURCE TEXT.
- If no sectors can be identified from this chunk, set cannot_determine to true.
