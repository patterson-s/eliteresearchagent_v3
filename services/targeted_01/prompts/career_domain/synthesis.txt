Step 1 — Jobs review

    academic / civil_society / corporate / diplomatic / political / international / other
  Separately tally primary roles (full-time employment) and secondary roles (advisory, board).
  Weight primary roles more heavily than secondary roles.

Step 2 — Duration weighting
  Where approximate periods are given, estimate the total years spent in each domain.
  Roles spanning many years should carry more weight than brief appointments.
  If duration data is sparse, rely on the primary/secondary distinction from Step 1.

Step 3 — Sectors cross-check
  From the Q5 sectors output, check whether the primary sector reinforces a particular domain:
    - "development" or "global_health" alongside NGO roles → civil_society signal
    - "finance" or "trade" alongside corporate or governmental roles → political or corporate signal
    - "science" or "education" alongside academic roles → academic signal
    - "diplomacy" or "international_law" alongside ambassadorial roles → diplomatic signal
  Note any alignment or tension between job domain and sector.

Step 4 — Networks cross-check
  From the Q6 networks output, check whether the affiliations and awards pattern reinforces
  the emerging domain classification:
    - Fellowships at academic institutes → academic signal
    - Board seats at corporations → corporate signal
    - Advisory roles to governments/multilateral bodies → political or international signal
    - Prizes and distinctions in a sector → reinforces that sector's domain

Step 5 — Dominant domain identification
  Based on Steps 1–4, determine which single domain best characterizes the overall career:
    "academic"      — career primarily in universities, research institutes, or academic bodies
    "civil_society" — career primarily in NGOs, foundations, think-tanks, civil society orgs
    "corporate"     — career primarily in private sector firms, banks, or consultancies
    "diplomatic"    — career primarily in bilateral or multilateral diplomatic service
    "political"     — career primarily in elected or appointed government positions
    "international" — career primarily in international organizations (UN, World Bank, IMF, etc.)
    "other"         — career does not fit any of the above categories
  Record this as dominant_domain.

Step 6 — Hybrid test
  A hybrid classification is appropriate ONLY when:
    - Two domains each account for a comparable share of the career (roughly 35%+ each), AND
    - The domains represent genuinely distinct institutional contexts (not just overlapping sectors)
  Examples of genuine hybrids: equal time in academia and politics; equal time in civil_society
  and international organizations.
  If the career has a clear dominant domain with some secondary exposure, do NOT classify as hybrid.
  Set is_hybrid to true only when the hybrid condition is clearly met.
  If is_hybrid is true, list both domains in hybrid_domains (exactly 2 entries).
