ROLE
You are a precision researcher extracting structured career data from biographical source text about international elites.

TASK
Identify the specific job title and organization held by {{PERSON_NAME}} at the time they were nominated to the following panel:

  Panel name : {{HLP_NAME}}
  Nomination year : {{NOMINATION_YEAR}}
  Person's age at nomination : {{NOMINATION_AGE}}

PERSON

SOURCE TEXT
{{CHUNK_TEXT}}

REASONING STEPS
Work through each step before producing your JSON output.

Step 1 — Temporal anchoring
  Does this text describe {{PERSON_NAME}}'s career around {{NOMINATION_YEAR}}?
  Look for: explicit years, approximate periods ("in the early 2010s", "at the time"), or
  transitional phrases such as "at the time of his/her appointment", "when nominated",
  "was serving as ... before joining", "held the position of ... until {{NOMINATION_YEAR}}".
  Note any year markers you find.

Step 2 — Role identification
  Does the text name a specific job title or role held by {{PERSON_NAME}}?
  Acceptable titles include: Professor, Minister, Director, CEO, Secretary-General, Chair,
  Managing Director, Special Adviser, Fellow, Permanent Representative, Ambassador, etc.
  Distinguish between the role of {{PERSON_NAME}} and roles held by other people mentioned
  in the same text.

Step 3 — Organization identification
  Does the text name the organization, institution, or employer linked to that role?
  Prefer the full official name over abbreviations when both appear in the text
  (e.g. "Massachusetts Institute of Technology" over "MIT" if both are present).

Step 4 — Temporal match assessment
  Is the identified role plausibly the one held at or immediately before {{NOMINATION_YEAR}}?
  - If the text gives an explicit date range for the role (e.g. "2010–2016"), does it
    overlap with or immediately precede {{NOMINATION_YEAR}}?
  - If the text describes a role that clearly began AFTER the panel (e.g. "following his
    service on the panel, he became..."), it is NOT the nomination-time role. Exclude it.
  - If the text describes a long-standing role with no end date and no contradicting
    information, it may still be the nomination-time role.

Step 5 — Confidence assessment
  Assign one of:
    "high"   — The text explicitly states or very strongly implies the role at nomination
               time. A year marker matches, or the text uses language like "was serving as X
               when appointed to the panel", or the role has an explicit date range covering
               {{NOMINATION_YEAR}}.
    "medium" — A role is mentioned near {{NOMINATION_YEAR}} but the temporal link is
               implicit or approximate (e.g. the role is listed in a biography section
               without exact dates, but is consistent with the nomination period).
    "low"    — A role is mentioned but there is no temporal information linking it to
               {{NOMINATION_YEAR}}, or the link is speculative.
  If no job title can be identified at all, set cannot_determine to true.

GUIDELINES
- Extract information about {{PERSON_NAME}} only. Ignore roles held by other people.
- Do not infer or fabricate titles. Only report what is explicitly stated or very directly
  implied by the source text.
- If the text mentions the panel itself, the nomination-time title is what {{PERSON_NAME}}
  held BEFORE or AT the moment of joining — not a title acquired afterward.
