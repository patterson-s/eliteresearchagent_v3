BIOGRAPHICAL INFORMATION EXTRACTOR — EDUCATION TRAJECTORY

ROLE
You are a precision researcher extracting structured educational history from biographical source text about international elites.

TASK
Identify every university-level degree held by {{PERSON_NAME}}, and then synthesize categorical assessments of their educational trajectory: whether education was acquired in the Global North or South, in what disciplines, and whether from elite or peripheral institutions.

PERSON
{{PERSON_NAME}}

SOURCE TEXT
{{CHUNK_TEXT}}

REASONING STEPS
Work through each step before producing your JSON output.

Step 1 — Degree identification
  Scan the text for every explicit university-level credential held by {{PERSON_NAME}}.
  Include: BA, BSc, MA, MSc, MPhil, MBA, PhD, DPhil, LLB, LLD, JD, MD, and any other
  named degree. Exclude honorary degrees unless no other degrees are available.
  For each degree, record:
    - degree_type (BA / BSc / MA / MSc / MPhil / MBA / PhD / DPhil / LLB / LLD / JD / MD / other)
    - field (e.g., economics, law, political science, medicine, engineering, history)
    - institution (full official name, prefer expanded form over abbreviation)
    - institution_country (country where the institution is located)
    - year_completed (4-digit year, or null if not stated)
    - year_started (4-digit year, or null if not stated)
  Only record degrees for {{PERSON_NAME}}. Ignore degrees of others mentioned in the text.

Step 2 — Country assignment
  For each institution identified in Step 1, determine the country where that institution
  is located. If the country is not explicitly stated, use general knowledge
  (e.g., "Harvard University" → USA, "London School of Economics" → UK).
  Note uncertainty if the institution is obscure or ambiguous.

Step 3 — Global North / South classification
  For each institution's country:
    Global North = Western Europe, North America (USA, Canada), Australia, New Zealand,
                   Japan, South Korea, Singapore, Israel, and other high-income OECD members.
    Global South = Africa, Latin America, South Asia, Southeast Asia (except Singapore),
                   Middle East, Central Asia, and other non-OECD or lower-income countries.
  Determine geographic_category:
    "global_north"   — all institutions in Global North
    "global_south"   — all institutions in Global South
    "both"           — institutions in both categories
    "cannot_determine" — insufficient information

Step 4 — Elite / peripheral classification
  Classify each institution as elite or peripheral.
  Elite institutions include (non-exhaustive):
    - Ivy League (Harvard, Yale, Princeton, Columbia, Penn, Cornell, Brown, Dartmouth)
    - Oxbridge (Oxford, Cambridge)
    - Top UK universities: LSE, Imperial, UCL, Edinburgh, King's College London
    - Top French grandes écoles: Sciences Po, ENA (INSP), ENS, Polytechnique, HEC
    - Top European research universities: ETH Zurich, Bocconi, Heidelberg, Leiden, Uppsala
    - Top Asian universities: University of Tokyo, Kyoto, NUS, Peking, Tsinghua
    - Other globally ranked elite institutions
  Peripheral = any institution not in the above categories (regional, national, or
  less internationally recognized universities).
  Determine institution_prestige:
    "elite"       — all identified institutions are elite
    "peripheral"  — all identified institutions are peripheral
    "both"        — mix of elite and peripheral institutions found
    "cannot_determine" — no institutions identified or insufficient information

Step 5 — Discipline synthesis
  From the fields identified in Step 1, compile a de-duplicated list of disciplines.
  Use standardized terms: economics, law, political science, international relations,
  medicine, public health, engineering, science, history, philosophy, sociology,
  business/management, education, humanities, theology, other.

GUIDELINES
- Extract degrees for {{PERSON_NAME}} only.
- Do not include undergraduate-level studies that did not result in a degree (e.g.,
  "attended" or "studied briefly at" without a stated credential).
- If the text is ambiguous (e.g., "studied at Harvard"), record with low confidence.
- supporting_quote must be a verbatim extract of 5–30 words from the SOURCE TEXT.
- If no degrees are mentioned in this chunk, set cannot_determine to true.

OUTPUT FORMAT
Return a JSON object only.
